1. What are the various modes of payment?
Foreign currency payment is possible only through wire transfer. Payment in Indian rupees can be made in any of the following forms:

a. Cheque
b. Draft
c. Pay Order
d. Banker's cheque
e. Wire Transfer
f. RTGS/NEFT
2. Does KMB facilitate in availing home loans?
We will help our customers by getting the necessary approvals for the project from the HFIs and coordinate with them (as developers) by submitting the necessary documents required. At present, HDFC and ICICI have pre approved our project.
3. We are applying for Bank loans. Since Banks will not process the same till we submit the sale agreements, how can you expect us to make the initial payments without generating the sale agreements?
Bank will process only 80% loan of the total cost. The customer has to pay the remaining 20% for which we raise the demand initially. However, the initial payment has to be made to enter into the agreement to sell.
4. What is the payment plan?
The standard payment plan consists of the following:

25% of total apartment cost (excluding outgoings, statutory payments, utility deposits etc,) to be paid before signing the Agreement to Sell. Out of this, a specified initial amount is to be paid as a “blocking amount” at the time of booking and the balance needed to make up 25% is to be paid within 30 days of blocking, post which an Agreement to sell will be signed.

75% of total apartment cost (excluding outgoings, statutory payments, utility deposits etc.,) in instalments, staggered over a period starting from signing of the Agreement to Sell and ending on completion of the Project. The last two instalments will be 10% and 5% respectively.

Over and above the total apartment cost, the following amounts are also payable upon completion of the Project and before delivery of possession:

a. Stamp Duty payable on the Agreement to Sell (at actual as per statutory regulations applicable at the time of signing of the Agreement to Sell). b. Stamp Duty and registration fees on the sale deed (at actual as per statutory regulations applicable at the time of registration of the sale deed). c. One year’s Maintenance Charges d. 4 years Advance maintenance deposit e. Lump sum Legal Charges f. Lump sum amount towards charges for Water and Electricity meter g. Lump sum amount towards Infrastructure Charges payable to BESCOM and BWSSB

Note:

Items (a) and (b) are payable by Demand Draft/Pay Order favouring the respective Government authority empowered to collect these payments.
Item (g) is payable in 4 instalments during the progress of construction
5. What is the capital gain?
Capital gain will accrue from the date of Sale Deed or possession whichever is earlier and if the property is sold within 3 years from the date of sale deed, the same will be treated as Short Term Capital Gain and if it is more than 3 years from the date of sale deed, the same will be treated as Long Term Capital Gain.
